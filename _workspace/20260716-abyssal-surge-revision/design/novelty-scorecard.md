# Abyssal Surge — storytelling novelty scorecard

## Candidate

**The silent commander's three-turn reveal:** command text and optional, still-led cues turn the unchanged three-stage campaign into a containment → prison → succession arc. The Warden never receives a new action, upgrade, stage, or spoken dialogue; each opponent supplies one concise line and each existing mechanical gate supplies the scene pressure.

| Dimension | Design rating (0–5) | Evidence / limitation |
|---|---:|---|
| Mechanical fit | 5 | Maps directly to hunt/extract/materialize/capture, Stage 2 possession, Stage 3 one-use Domain, and Assault. AS-WV-004–010, AS-WV-074. |
| Narrative coherence | 4 | One question per stage and one turn per opponent; success depends on the optional cues being concise and consistent. AS-WV-070–073. |
| Player agency | 4 | Reward selection remains meaningful but story copy cannot imply a mechanically different ending. AS-WV-016–043. |
| Production feasibility | 3 | Text-only path is feasible; optional still/video/audio requires resource provenance, source ownership, and QA validation. |
| Accessibility / fallback | 4 | The core loop is readable without media; Korean text remains the canonical narrative fallback. AS-WV-044–066. |
| Novelty risk | 3 | “Succession” can be mistaken for a promised sequel or fourth stage; the final copy must say campaign complete and make no such promise. |

**Score meaning:** this is a preproduction judgment, not user research. No score is evidence of player preference, comprehension, or commercial value.

## Testable hypothesis and decision rule

| Field | Contract |
|---|---|
| Hypothesis | Showing the traceable entry/boss cues will increase stage-objective + story-turn recall without decreasing task completion or forcing media use. |
| Comparison | Same deterministic build: narrative-cue condition versus text-suppressed presentation condition. No mechanics, reward effects, timing rules, or commands differ. |
| Measures | For each completed stage: objective recall, three-choice turn recognition, completion, elapsed time, skip/media-fallback use, and reported confusion. |
| Success threshold | With sample size recorded, ≥70% in the cue condition correctly state the mandatory objective **and** identify the intended turn; completion and median elapsed time do not regress against the comparison condition. |
| Guardrail | A participant who skips/unavailable-media falls back to the normal text/UI path and must remain able to finish. |
| Status | **NOT-RUN.** No sample, result, confidence interval, or ship decision exists. |

## Method reference

The design uses the webtoon-harness scenario principles—dialogue-led tension, a stage turn, and an edited continuity pass—as a campaign-writing method, not as a requirement to generate a webtoon. Source: [revfactory/webtoon-harness](https://github.com/revfactory/webtoon-harness), consulted **2026-07-16**. No external gameplay-performance claim is derived from that source.
